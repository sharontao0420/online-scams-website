A Real Case Told by Charelle

"As a civil servant for the City of New Orleans, I experienced a cyber attack. On my work computer it was obvious that there was some sort of virus working on the system and when I realized that my coworkers were experiencing the same strange dialog boxes and operation errors, I informed them to restart their systems. It was soon after this restart that we received communication from our IT department to shut down all computers. As detailed in this article, we were also informed to disconnect our systems from the network."

Extra precautions had to be taken

The process of recovery was long and luckily quick thinking on the part of our IT professionals ensured that our information was protected and recoverable, but the situation limited our abilities for for months and extra precautions had to be taken such:

  • closing the network operations to within network only

  • Ensuring all computers were updated and receive regular updates

  • Strengthening of emails

  • Consistent training

  • And faux phishing attacks to ensure employees are vigilant.

Further Exploration From the Real Case

The primary way users are attacked is through their emails. Attackers send emails pretending to be a trusted source and convince users to do one of the following:

  • Click a link that will download malicious software to the system

  • Obtain login or password information by requesting users supply this information under false pretenses.

  • Gaining the users confidence by pretending to be a trusted peer and requesting funds by gift card, check, etc.


Internet Crime Report

People report crimes to www.ic3.gov and these complaints are logged by the FBI. The report is released every year to bring public awareness. In 2021, due to a rise in Elder Fraud, they plan to release their first dedicated report on scams targeting the elderly.

Elderly is defined as victims over the age of 60 and they are targeted because they are viewed as persons with significant financial resources. Age demographic is not a required field in complaint reports, so numbers are based on those who have identified themselves as over 60. Roughly over $966 million losses in revenue are recorded for those identified in this group.

IC3 RAT (Recovery Asset Team)

IC3 RAT (Recovery Asset Team) works to freeze funds for victims of fraudulent funds transferred to domestic accounts. In 2020, the report highlighted fraud in relation to these categories: COVID-19 Aid, Business Email Compromise (BEC), Tech Support Fraud, and Ransomware.
